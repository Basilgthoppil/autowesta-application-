About AutoWesta

The Problem

Agriculture remains the backbone of our economy, yet many farmers lack access to precise, localized weather data. Existing commercial weather stations and advanced forecasting technologies are often prohibitively expensive—putting them entirely out of reach for small-to-medium scale farmers. This lack of data makes crops vulnerable to unpredictable weather patterns, inefficient irrigation routines, and preventable diseases, directly impacting yields and livelihoods.

The Solution

AutoWesta was developed to democratize agricultural telemetry. By engineering an affordable, interconnected weather station that tracks vital localized metrics (Temperature, Humidity, Wind, Rainfall, and Pressure), farmers gain unprecedented visibility into their micro-climates. Furthermore, AutoWesta integrates state-of-the-art Artificial Intelligence (Conv1D-LSTM architecture) hosted on the Hugging Face Cloud to actively predict environmental shifts 7 days in advance. This empowers farmers to preemptively protect crops, optimize water usage, and maximize their harvests—all at a fraction of the cost of traditional enterprise solutions.

The Team

AutoWesta is the capstone research project developed by five dedicated final-year Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE) students from the TocH Institute of Science and Technology.
